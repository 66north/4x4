P0700 — Transmission Control System Malfunction

● Intermediate ⏱ 60 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

P0700 is a generic alert code meaning the Transmission Control Module (TCM) has detected a fault and stored its own fault code. P0700 itself does not describe the specific fault — it is the TCM signalling the ECU that there is a transmission problem. You must read the TCM fault codes separately using an OBD-II scanner that supports enhanced (manufacturer-specific) codes. Common companion codes include P0715 (input speed sensor), P0720 (output speed sensor), and P0750–P0770 (solenoid faults).

When do you need this?

  • Check Engine light illuminated
  • Transmission warning light (if equipped)
  • Harsh or erratic gear shifts
  • Transmission stuck in single gear (limp mode)
  • Delayed engagement when selecting Drive or Reverse
  • Slipping between gears

Tips & common mistakes

  • P0700 alone tells you nothing specific — you must read the TCM's own codes with a scanner that supports Mitsubishi enhanced codes (not just generic OBD-II).
  • Transmission fluid degradation is the most common cause. If fluid is dark brown or smells burnt, a full fluid and filter change may resolve the issue.
  • Limp mode (stuck in 3rd gear) is the TCM protecting the transmission from further damage. Do not ignore it.
  • A basic OBD-II scanner may show P0700 but not the specific sub-codes. A Mitsubishi-compatible scanner (or Snap-on/Autel with enhanced support) is needed.
  • If the transmission worked perfectly before a fluid change and now has issues, the wrong fluid type was likely used. ATF SP-III is required — not generic Dexron III.
