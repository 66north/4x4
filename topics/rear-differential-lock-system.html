Rear Differential Lock — Air Pump & Diagnosis

● Intermediate ⏱ 60 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

The Pajero IV's rear differential has an optional air-operated locking differential that allows both rear wheels to spin together for maximum traction in deep mud or rock crawling. The system uses a 12V DC pump, air lines, and a solenoid-operated air lock chamber. Without the lock engaged, the differential spins wheels independently (normal on-road mode). The lock can fail silently — the light on the dash shows status, but diagnosis requires checking air pressure, pump operation, and electrical signals. A seized or failed air pump is the most common fault.

When do you need this?

  • Differential lock light on the dashboard flashing or not responding after selector
  • Lock engages but will not disengage (stuck open)
  • Hissing or whistling from the rear axle area when lock attempts engagement
  • Rear diff lock unavailable during off-road operation even though selector was moved
  • Slow response to lock engagement request (pump sluggish or weak)
  • Grinding noise when lock engages or disengages (mechanical seizure)

Tools needed

  • Multimeter (to check voltage at pump and solenoid)
  • OBD-II scanner (to read differential lock control codes)
  • Air pressure gauge (to test pump output pressure)
  • 13mm socket (solenoid and detection switch removal)

Tips & common mistakes

  • Check the dashboard indicator first: solid light means lock is engaged, flashing means lock signal is being sent but mechanical engagement is incomplete, no light means electrical signal not reaching the solenoid.
  • The rear differential lock is a luxury feature, not essential for normal driving — if it fails, the vehicle remains safely drivable in 2H or 4H without locking.
  • The air pump draws power directly from the battery through a relay in the engine bay fuse box. A blown fuse cuts power to the pump immediately — check this before assuming pump failure.
  • To test the pump: activate the selector (you will hear a brief 'click' in the engine bay as the relay closes). If no sound and the indicator light doesn't come on, the relay or fuse has failed.
  • If the pump runs (audible whining in the engine bay) but the light doesn't illuminate, the solenoid on the rear axle is not receiving signal — check the differential lock detection switch (located on the rear axle assembly).
  • Air line failure: if a line has cracked or detached, air pressure will be lost and the lock will not hold. Inspect lines running along the frame from the pump to the rear axle. Listen for hissing with the lock engaged.
  • Do not attempt to drive aggressively on-road with the lock engaged — locking the differential puts extreme stress on driveshafts and suspension. Lock should only be engaged when off-road or in extreme low-speed conditions (below 5 km/h).
  • After off-road use, always unlock the differential immediately on solid ground before returning to paved roads — continuous locking on asphalt causes tyre scrub and driveline damage.
