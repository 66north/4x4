Air Conditioning Recharge (Regas)

● Intermediate ⏱ 60 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

The Pajero IV AC system uses R134a refrigerant. A recharge (regas) restores cooling performance when refrigerant has leaked out over time. This is a workshop job — the system must be evacuated, leak-tested, and charged to the exact specification weight. Overcharging is as damaging as undercharging. Do not use DIY top-up cans without confirming there is no active leak.

When do you need this?

  • AC gradually losing cooling performance over months or years
  • Compressor cycling rapidly (low-pressure cutout activating)
  • AC vents blowing slightly cool but not cold
  • Previously recharged system that has lost performance again

Tips & common mistakes

  • Always fix the leak before recharging. A regas on a leaking system is money wasted — the gas will be gone again within weeks or months.
  • The refrigerant charge weight label is on a sticker in the engine bay near the AC components. Australian/Middle East variants may differ from European spec.
  • If the system has been empty for more than a few months, replace the receiver-drier at the same time — moisture contamination damages the compressor.
  • A workshop regas with a proper machine costs $80–150 and includes vacuum test, leak check, and accurate charge. This is the only correct way to recharge AC.
  • Clicking or rattling from the AC compressor under load indicates bearing wear — stop using the AC and have the compressor inspected before it seizes and destroys the system.
  • Dual-zone systems (front + rear) share the same refrigerant loop but have independent blower motors. If only one zone fails, check that zone's blower before assuming a refrigerant fault.
