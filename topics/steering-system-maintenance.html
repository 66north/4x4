Steering System Maintenance

● Moderate ⏱ 30 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

Steering system maintenance on the Pajero IV covers the power steering fluid, steering rack boots, tie rod ends, and steering damper. These components are inspected at major services but rarely replaced on schedule — most are replaced when they show wear. A well-maintained steering system gives precise, responsive handling; neglected components cause wandering, play, and vibration.

When do you need this?

  • Excessive play or looseness in the steering wheel (worn tie rods or rack)
  • Vehicle wanders on straight roads (alignment or worn steering components)
  • Clunking over bumps from steering area (worn steering damper or ball joints)
  • Torn steering rack boots (inspect visually at every service)

Tools needed

  • Torque wrench
  • 17mm, 19mm spanners (tie rod lock nut)
  • Ball joint separator / pickle fork

Torque specifications

  • Tie rod end lock nut: 60 N·m — 55–65 Nm after alignment. Recheck after 500 km.
  • Tie rod end castle nut (to steering knuckle): 35 N·m — 30–40 Nm then align to cotter pin hole.

Tips & common mistakes

  • Always get a wheel alignment after replacing tie rod ends — even a small change in tie rod length shifts the toe setting.
  • Check tie rod end play by gripping the tyre at 9 and 3 o'clock and pushing/pulling. Any detectable knock is a worn end — replace it.
  • Steering rack boots protect the rack from grit and moisture. Torn boots lead to rack seal failure within 12–24 months. They cost $15 to replace.
  • The steering damper is often forgotten — it is located across the front axle and dampens kickback from rough terrain. A failed damper causes steering wheel wobble on corrugated roads.
