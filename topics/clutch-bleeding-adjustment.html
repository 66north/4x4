Clutch Bleeding & Pedal Adjustment

● Moderate ⏱ 45 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

Manual transmission Pajero IV models use a hydraulic clutch with a master cylinder at the pedal and a slave cylinder at the gearbox. Air in the hydraulic circuit causes a spongy pedal and incomplete clutch release — symptoms identical to a worn clutch plate. Bleeding removes air from the circuit. Pedal height and freeplay also have factory specifications and affect clutch feel and release point. These checks should be part of any clutch complaint diagnosis before assuming mechanical wear.

When do you need this?

  • Spongy or soft clutch pedal (air in hydraulic circuit)
  • Clutch not fully releasing — hard to select gears from rest (air or low fluid)
  • Clutch pedal height changed or feels different to normal
  • Clutch fluid level dropping in reservoir (leak in master or slave cylinder)
  • Clutch drag after fluid change or any hydraulic work

Tools needed

  • Clutch bleeding kit or vacuum bleeder
  • 8mm spanner (bleed nipple on slave cylinder)
  • Clear plastic tube and catch bottle
  • Torque wrench

Torque specifications

  • Clutch master cylinder mounting nuts: 13 N·m — 12–15 Nm.
  • Slave cylinder bleed nipple: 8 N·m — Do not overtighten — nipple is soft brass.

Tips & common mistakes

  • Check the clutch fluid reservoir before bleeding — it is usually the same reservoir as the brake master cylinder, or a separate small reservoir on the firewall near the clutch pedal. Level should be between MIN and MAX.
  • Bleeding procedure: attach a clear tube to the slave cylinder bleed nipple, open the nipple 3/4 turn, then pump the clutch pedal slowly to the floor and hold it. Close the nipple before releasing the pedal. Repeat until no bubbles appear in the fluid stream.
  • Never let the reservoir run dry while bleeding — air drawn in at the master cylinder will require you to start over.
  • Clutch pedal height (from floor to pedal pad): factory specification is approximately 170–180 mm. Adjust via the pedal stopper bolt behind the pedal arm.
  • Clutch pedal freeplay (slack before resistance is felt): approximately 1–3 mm. Adjust via the pushrod at the master cylinder.
  • If the clutch still drags after a thorough bleed with no air in the system, the fault is mechanical — worn pressure plate, bent clutch plate, or worn release bearing.
  • DOT 3/4 brake fluid absorbs moisture over time. If the fluid is more than 2 years old, replace it completely during the bleed rather than just topping up.
