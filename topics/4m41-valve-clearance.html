4M41 Diesel — Valve Clearance Check and Adjustment

● Intermediate ⏱ 90 min Gen 3 (2000–2006) Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 2000–2013

Valve clearance (also called valve lash) on the 4M41 diesel must be checked and adjusted with the engine cold. Correct clearance is critical for engine longevity and performance — too tight causes burnt valves, too loose causes noise and power loss. The 4M41 uses a rocker-arm design with an adjusting screw and lock nut, making adjustment straightforward. Allow at least 2 hours for the engine to cool before starting.

When do you need this?

  • Ticking or clattering noise from the top of the engine, especially when cold
  • Noise that disappears or changes as the engine warms up
  • Rough idle or slight power loss (if clearance is significantly out of spec)
  • Valve train noise at idle that increases with engine speed

Tools needed

  • Feeler gauge set — must include 0.10 mm and 0.15 mm blades
  • Open-end spanner 10 mm (lock nut)
  • Flat-blade screwdriver (adjusting screw)
  • Ratchet and socket set for rocker cover bolts
  • Crankshaft turning tool or large socket to rotate engine by hand
  • Torque wrench (lock nut tightening)

Torque specifications

  • : N·m — Hold adjusting screw stationary while tightening

Tips & common mistakes

  • The engine MUST be cold for accurate measurement. Heat expands metal and will give false readings.
  • When in doubt about which direction the camshaft projection is facing, rotate slowly and watch the rockers move — the cylinder that has just completed its intake stroke is on compression TDC.
  • If the feeler gauge drops in freely with no resistance, the gap is too wide and needs adjusting.
  • After tightening the lock nut, always recheck the clearance — the screw sometimes moves slightly when locked.
  • Replace the rocker cover gasket if it shows any hardening, cracking, or signs of leaking.
  • The 4M41 in Gen 3 (non-common rail) and Gen 4 (common rail) share the same valve clearance specification.
