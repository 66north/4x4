Rear Axle Bearing & Hub Inspection

● Moderate ⏱ 45 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

The Pajero IV rear axle has sealed hub bearings that support the wheel and transmit drive from the differential to each wheel. The bearings are sealed for life — they cannot be repacked, but can be inspected for play and wear. Bearing failure is rare under normal driving but increases with off-road abuse. Symptoms are a grinding noise when cornering or a rhythmic clicking/clunking at highway speed. Hub bearing inspection is simple and should be part of every major service, especially after off-road trips or high-mileage accumulation (150,000+ km).

When do you need this?

  • Grinding or growling noise from rear axle that changes pitch with speed
  • Clicking or popping sound during hard cornering (wheel bearing loading)
  • Vibration through the chassis that increases with speed (wheel imbalance or bearing play)
  • Loose or notchy feeling in the wheel when spun by hand (bearing preload worn)
  • Excessive play in the wheel when lifted (check lateral movement at the 3 and 9 o'clock positions)
  • Dust or grease around the wheel seal area (bearing seal failure — internal contamination)

Tools needed

  • Jack and jack stands (to safely lift the vehicle)
  • Wrench or socket for wheel bolts (usually 17mm)
  • Torque wrench

Torque specifications

  • Rear wheel bearing preload check: 0 N·m — Spin the wheel by hand after jacking up the vehicle. Should rotate freely with slight resistance (not binding or grinding). If notchy or gritty, bearing is suspect.
  • Wheel hub bolts: 108 N·m — 108–118 Nm. Torque in a star pattern after wheel installation.

Tips & common mistakes

  • Wheel bearing inspection is a visual and tactile check — you are feeling for play and listening for noise, not measuring anything with a dial gauge.
  • Jack the rear axle (not a wheel), support with jack stands under the frame, and spin each wheel by hand. Listen for grinding. Grasp the wheel at 3 and 9 o'clock and try to wiggle it — there should be no play (wheel should not move side-to-side).
  • A small amount of up-and-down play is normal (less than 2mm), but any side-to-side movement or grinding noise indicates bearing wear.
  • The sealed bearing cartridge is a complete unit — there is no adjustment or maintenance. If play is detected, the hub assembly must be replaced by pressing out the old bearing and pressing in a new one. This is a workshop job.
  • After off-road driving, check bearings more frequently (every 20,000 km) — mud, water, and rough terrain can accelerate bearing wear.
  • Do not ignore early warning signs: a slight grinding that comes and goes may suddenly worsen during a long highway drive, leading to a catastrophic failure with the wheel locking up.
  • Never attempt to open or repack a sealed hub bearing. If you suspect bearing damage, the hub must be replaced as a complete assembly. Partial disassembly will destroy the bearing immediately.
