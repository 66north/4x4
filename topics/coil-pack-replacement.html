Ignition Coil Pack Replacement

● Moderate ⏱ 30 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 6G75 2008–2013

The 6G75 petrol engine uses six individual coil-on-plug ignition coils, one per cylinder. Each coil sits directly on top of its spark plug. A failing coil causes a single-cylinder misfire (P0301–P0306) and rough running. Unlike older distributor systems, you can swap coils between cylinders to confirm a fault — if the misfire code follows the coil, the coil is bad. This is a petrol-only job; the 4M41 diesel has no spark ignition.

When do you need this?

  • P0301–P0306 misfire code (specific cylinder)
  • Rough idle or engine shaking
  • Hesitation or stumble at low RPM
  • Check Engine light illuminated
  • Symptoms worse when cold or damp

Tools needed

  • 10mm socket (coil retaining bolt)
  • Electrical connector removal tool (or small flat-head)
  • OBD-II scanner (to read and clear misfire codes)

Torque specifications

  • Coil pack retaining bolt: 10 N·m — 8–12 Nm.

Tips & common mistakes

  • To confirm a bad coil without buying parts: swap the suspect coil with an adjacent one and rescan. If P0302 becomes P0303, the coil is confirmed faulty.
  • Inspect the coil boot (rubber tip) for cracks or carbon tracking — a cracked boot can cause a misfire without the coil itself failing.
  • Coils are prone to failure after water ingress (engine washing, heavy rain). Misfire codes appearing after wet weather often mean a failed coil.
  • At 100,000+ km, consider replacing all 6 coils as a set. The labour to reach them is the same whether you replace one or six.
  • Always replace spark plugs at the same time as coils — worn plugs stress coils and cause premature failure.
