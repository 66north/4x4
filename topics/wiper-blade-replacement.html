Wiper Blade Replacement

● Easy ⏱ 15 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

The Pajero IV uses hook-type wiper arms. Replace blades when they streak, chatter, or leave unwiped bands — typically every 12–18 months or sooner in harsh climates. The front uses two different-length blades; the rear uses a shorter blade with a different attachment. Always replace front blades as a pair — a new driver blade against an old passenger blade causes uneven clearing.

When do you need this?

  • Streaking or smearing across the windscreen during wiping
  • Chattering or juddering as the blade crosses the glass
  • Unwiped arc or band left on the windscreen
  • Blade sitting away from glass at one end (split or hardened rubber)
  • Squealing wiper noise at low speed

Tools needed

  • No tools required — blade clips by hand

Tips & common mistakes

  • Lift the arm fully away from the glass before removing the blade — if it springs back without a blade fitted it can crack the windscreen.
  • Lay a folded cloth on the bonnet under the arm before lifting, as insurance if it drops.
  • The release tab is on the underside of the blade where it joins the arm hook. Press it toward the glass and slide the blade down and off.
  • Clean the windscreen with glass cleaner and the wiper arm hook with a rag before fitting the new blade — a gritty hook scratches the new blade insert immediately.
  • If new blades still streak, the windscreen may have a silicone or wax film from car wash products. Clean with an isopropyl alcohol wipe before wiping down with the new blade.
  • Rear wiper blades on the Pajero IV use the same hook fitting as the front — confirm the arm style before ordering, as some late-model vehicles use a pinch-tab adapter.
