Fuse Box Layout & Fuse Ratings

● Easy ⏱ 10 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

Fuse box locations and fuse assignments for the 2008–2013 Pajero IV. There are two fuse boxes: the engine bay fuse/relay box (left side of engine bay) and the interior fuse box (behind the glovebox panel, passenger side). Always replace a blown fuse with the identical amperage rating — a fuse that keeps blowing indicates an electrical fault, not a weak fuse.

When do you need this?

  • Electrical component stopped working suddenly (lights, windows, wipers, accessories)
  • Check Engine or warning light on for an electrical system
  • Intermittent fault that clears itself (borderline-blown fuse)

Tips & common mistakes

  • The engine bay fuse box cover has a fuse layout diagram printed on the inside of the lid — use it before consulting the manual.
  • The interior fuse box is located behind the lower glovebox panel. Open the glovebox, press the side clips to drop it down, and the fuse box is above.
  • A fuse that blows repeatedly means there is a short circuit downstream. Replacing with a higher-rated fuse is dangerous — do not do it.
  • Check fuses visually: the thin metal bridge inside the fuse should be intact. Some fuses look fine visually but are blown internally — test with a multimeter if unsure.
  • Tail light, interior light, and accessory socket faults are almost always a blown fuse before anything more complex.
  • If a fuse blows immediately on replacement, unplug the load (lamp, accessory) first to confirm it is the fault source before replacing the fuse again.
