Transfer Case Oil Change

● Moderate ⏱ 45 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

The transfer case distributes drive to front and rear axles and enables low-range 4WD. It uses its own fluid, separate from the transmission. The Pajero IV transfer case takes approximately 1.1L of ATF SP-III (same fluid as the automatic transmission). This is the most commonly forgotten fluid service on 4WD vehicles — neglect causes premature wear of the chain or gear set inside. Service interval: every 40,000 km or whenever the fluid is dark or contaminated.

When do you need this?

  • Routine service interval reached (40,000 km)
  • Vibration or whine from transfer case area in 4WD
  • 4WD engagement grinding or hesitation
  • Dark or metallic-looking fluid found on inspection

Tools needed

  • 17mm hex key / Allen socket (drain and fill plugs)
  • Drain pan (minimum 2L)
  • Torque wrench
  • Hand-operated fluid pump (for refilling through the side filler hole)

Torque specifications

  • Transfer case drain plug: 35 N·m — 33–38 Nm with new washer.
  • Transfer case filler plug: 35 N·m — 33–38 Nm.

Tips & common mistakes

  • The transfer case filler plug and drain plug are both hex-socket type (17mm Allen key). Do not confuse with the transmission pan bolts.
  • Fill until fluid runs out of the filler hole — this is the correct level. Do not try to estimate volume.
  • If the drained fluid contains metallic particles or looks like grey sludge, inspect the transfer case for damage before refilling.
  • Always check that the filler plug is removable before draining — if it is seized you cannot refill.
  • The transfer case is located behind the transmission and is accessible from underneath with the vehicle on ramps or a lift.
  • If you drive in water crossings or off-road mud, change the transfer case fluid every 20,000 km — water contamination degrades the fluid rapidly.
