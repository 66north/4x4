4M40 Diesel — Injection Pump Top Seal and Governor Cover Repair

● Hard ⏱ 150 min Gen 2 (1991–1999) 4M40 1991–1999

The Zexel (formerly Diesel Kiki) injection pump fitted to the 4M40 diesel (2.5 TDi and 2.8 TDi) has a known weakness: the governor cover top-plate seal rolls over under continuous fuel pressure, allowing fuel to seep from the top of the pump. The replacement part (updated design with moulded nibs to prevent rolling) is available in the Zexel repair kit and can be replaced with the pump on the engine — no pump removal or re-calibration is required. This procedure also covers the throttle shaft O-ring, which is the other common leak point. Do NOT confuse this with the front shaft seal (which does require pump removal and diesel-specialist recalibration).

When do you need this?

  • Fuel odour from the engine bay, especially after driving
  • Visible fuel staining or wet area on top of the injection pump
  • Fuel seeping around the governor cover (top plate) or throttle shaft
  • Fuel consumption higher than usual in severe cases

Tools needed

  • 5 mm allen key / hex socket (for top cover cap screws)
  • 10 mm combination spanner (throttle arm nut)
  • 17 mm combination spanner (optional, useful)
  • 6-inch thin steel rule (for guiding linkage during reassembly)
  • Needle-nose pliers
  • Two small flat-blade screwdrivers
  • Felt-tip marker or Tipp-Ex correction fluid (for alignment marks)
  • Piece of string (reassembly aid — no knots)
  • Degreaser and hose-down water
  • Rag and drain tray

Tips & common mistakes

  • The string trick for reassembly is the hardest part — take time to get this right. A dropped string inside the pump means disassembly again.
  • If you damaged the governor cover seal groove with a screwdriver during removal, the new seal may not seal correctly. Clean up with a fine file if needed.
  • Do not disturb the idle speed or maximum fuel stop screws unless necessary — they are factory calibrated and difficult to set correctly by eye.
  • The front shaft seal (where the pump drive shaft enters) requires pump removal and bench work by a diesel specialist. Do not attempt it in situ.
  • After the repair, run the engine and check thoroughly for any remaining leaks. If the top is now dry but the front is wet, you have a front seal leak — different job.
  • Zexel kit 146600-1120 is widely available from diesel injection specialists. A complete kit is worth having even if you only use a few parts.
