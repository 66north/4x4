Hose Part Numbers — Coolant, Vacuum & Brake Hose Reference

● Moderate ⏱ 20 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

OEM and replacement hose part numbers for the 2008–2013 Pajero IV. Covers coolant hoses (upper 32mm, lower 38mm), vacuum hoses (4–8mm ID), and brake hoses. Hoses degrade with heat and UV over 5–10 years and should be inspected at every major service. A cracked vacuum hose triggers multiple fault codes; a failed brake hose is a safety emergency. Replace before failure, not after.

Tools needed

  • Hose clamp pliers (for spring clamps)
  • Adjustable wrench and screwdrivers (for worm-drive clamps)
  • Coolant drain pan (minimum 12L capacity)
  • Brake bleeder kit (for brake hose replacement)
  • 14mm brake line wrench (flare nut wrench — not open-ended)
  • Torque wrench

Torque specifications

  • Banjo bolt (brake hose to caliper): 30 N·m — 27–35 Nm. Always use new crush washers.
  • Coolant hose clamp (worm-drive): 4 N·m — 3–5 Nm. Snug only — over-tightening cuts the hose.

Tips & common mistakes

  • Brake hose failure is a safety emergency — spongy or uneven pedal after hose inspection means immediate replacement.
  • Always bleed the brakes after replacing any brake hose. Air in the lines causes soft pedal that won't firm up under hard braking.
  • Vacuum hose cracks are invisible until they cause a fault code. Inspect by feel — a healthy hose is flexible but firm; a degraded one is crumbly or stiff.
  • Coolant hoses soften and swell internally before they fail externally. Squeeze the upper hose when cold — if it collapses instead of springing back, replace it.
  • OEM hose part numbers above should be verified against your VIN at the dealer — some variants have minor routing differences.
  • Replace coolant hoses and clamps as a set when doing a major coolant flush — labour cost is the same and hose clamps are cheap insurance.
