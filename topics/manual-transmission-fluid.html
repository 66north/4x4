Manual Transmission Fluid — Check & Replacement

● Moderate ⏱ 45 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

Manual transmission Pajero IV models use a separate gear oil for the gearbox, distinct from the transfer case fluid. The gearbox oil lubricates the gear sets, synchronisers, and bearings. It is not changed at regular intervals like engine oil — Mitsubishi treats it as a lifetime fill, but in practice the fluid degrades through heat cycling and moisture absorption, especially in vehicles used off-road or for towing. Inspection at 80,000 km and replacement every 80,000– 100,000 km is good preventive maintenance. Worn or contaminated fluid accelerates synchro wear and causes hard gear selection.

When do you need this?

  • Routine service interval (80,000–100,000 km)
  • Hard or notchy gear changes, especially when cold
  • Metallic contamination or dark colour found when inspecting fluid
  • Gear whine or noise during acceleration (indicating oil starvation)
  • Difficulty engaging specific gears (synchro wear exacerbated by low or degraded fluid)

Tools needed

  • 17mm hex key / Allen socket (drain and filler plugs)
  • Drain pan (minimum 3L)
  • Torque wrench
  • Hand-operated fluid pump (for refilling through the side filler hole)

Torque specifications

  • Gearbox drain plug: 35 N·m — 33–38 Nm with new copper washer.
  • Gearbox filler plug: 35 N·m — 33–38 Nm. Confirm filler plug is removable before draining.

Tips & common mistakes

  • Always confirm the filler plug can be removed before draining — if it is seized you will not be able to refill.
  • The filler/level hole is on the side of the gearbox casing, typically at mid-height. Fill until fluid drips from the filler hole — this is the correct level. Do not estimate by volume.
  • Manual gearbox fluid is different from the transfer case fluid. Both have separate drain and filler plugs. The gearbox is forward in the drivetrain; the transfer case is behind it. Do not confuse the two.
  • GL-4 and GL-5 are NOT interchangeable. GL-5 additives (extreme pressure sulphur compounds) corrode yellow metal alloys used in synchroniser rings on many manual gearboxes. Use GL-4 unless the manual specifies GL-5 or GL-4/5 dual-rated fluid.
  • If the drained fluid is dark, smells burnt, or contains metallic particles, inspect the drain plug magnet carefully. Fine metallic fuzz is normal wear; larger flakes indicate accelerated component wear and the gearbox should be inspected before assuming fluid replacement alone will fix it.
  • After an off-road water crossing (especially where the gearbox was submerged), change the fluid as soon as possible. Water contamination destroys synchronisers rapidly — water-contaminated gear oil looks milky or has a grey tint.
