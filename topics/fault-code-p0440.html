P0440 — Evaporative Emission Control System Malfunction

● Moderate ⏱ 45 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 6G75 2008–2013

P0440 indicates a large leak or malfunction in the EVAP (evaporative emission control) system. This system captures fuel vapour from the fuel tank and routes it into the intake manifold to be burned rather than venting to atmosphere. The most common cause is a loose or damaged fuel filler cap — always start there. This is a petrol-only code; the 4M41 diesel does not have an EVAP system.

When do you need this?

  • Check Engine light illuminated
  • Smell of fuel from outside the vehicle (fuel vapour escaping)
  • No drivability symptoms in most cases
  • Failed emissions test

Tips & common mistakes

  • Always start with the fuel filler cap — it is free to inspect and $15–40 to replace. It fixes P0440 in 40% of cases.
  • Tighten the cap until it clicks. Many drivers tighten it too loosely, causing intermittent P0440.
  • If the fuel smell is strong and persistent, suspect a cracked canister or disconnected hose — this is also a fire hazard and should be fixed promptly.
  • P0440 does not affect engine performance. The vehicle is safe to drive while diagnosing, but should not be ignored due to emissions and fire risk.
