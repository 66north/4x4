Differential Oil Change (Front & Rear)

● Intermediate ⏱ 90 min Gen 4 (2007–2013) 4M41 6G75 2008–2013

The front and rear differentials contain hypoid gear oil that lubricates the ring and pinion gears and the differential carrier. Over time, the oil oxidizes and metallic particles from gear wear accumulate, reducing lubricant viscosity and protective film thickness. Factory intervals call for replacement every 40,000 km under normal driving, or every 20,000 km for towing or off-road use. Neglecting differential oil changes causes accelerated gear wear, whining noises, and eventual catastrophic failure.

When do you need this?

  • Scheduled maintenance interval (40,000 km normal, 20,000 km if towing/off-road)
  • Whining or howling from the differential during acceleration or coasting
  • Metallic grinding noise when turning (suggests gear damage)
  • Visible contamination in oil when draining (metal shavings, sludge)
  • Oil overfilled or low level (indicates internal leakage or overflow)
  • Sudden loss of traction or clunking on hard acceleration

Tools needed

  • Axle/differential drain pan (minimum 3 liters capacity)
  • Wrench or socket for drain plugs (usually 17mm or 19mm hex)
  • Wrench or socket for filler plugs (usually 24mm)
  • New crush washers or gaskets (buy with the oil)
  • Hand pump or electric oil pump (to refill the differential — very slow with a funnel)
  • Clean rags or shop towels (to wipe plugs and sealing surfaces)
  • Jack and jack stands (to safely lift the vehicle off the ground)

Tips & common mistakes

  • The front differential is on the front axle, under the engine bay. The rear differential is visible on the rear axle assembly, mounted to the frame.
  • If the vehicle has multiple transfer case settings (2H, 4H, 4L), select 2H (high-range 2WD) before starting work. Do not change gears while the vehicle is elevated.
  • Drain the front differential first, then the rear. Let each drain for at least 10 minutes to ensure full evacuation.
  • The filler plug is located above the drain plug (on the side of the differential housing). Once drained, remove this plug to check the level after refilling.
  • Filling via a regular funnel is tedious; an electric oil pump with a tube makes the job 10x faster. Pour the oil into the pump bottle, position the tube in the filler hole, and cycle the pump.
  • The differential should be filled until oil just reaches the bottom of the filler plug hole. Use a straight edge or rod to measure this precisely.
  • Reinstall the filler plug before the drain plug is fully tightened — this ensures you can remove it for level verification.
  • After refilling, idle the vehicle in neutral for 1–2 minutes, then drive gently for 10 km. Recheck the level; it may need topping up if air pockets were purged.
  • Some enthusiasts use a catch-can to capture a small oil sample during draining. Examine it under light for the volume and size of metal particles — this indicates gear wear.
  • If the differential oil is severely contaminated (black, chunky sludge) or metallic particles are visible, a deeper inspection or gearbox overhaul may be warranted.
