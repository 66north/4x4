BASE ENGINE


CYLINDER HEAD




The cylinder head is lightweight and uses aluminium alloy with high cooling efficiency.
Compared to the conventional 6G75 engine, the port shape is optimized and the shape of water hole on lower side is change to conform to the single oil feeder control valve.
The intake and exhaust ports are of cross flow type. 2 each are independently provided on the left and right.

VALVE SEAT




Sintered alloy valve seat is used.

VALVE GUIDE


Sintered alloy valve guide is used.

CYLINDER HEAD GASKET




To facilitate heat resistance and improve sealing performance, double-layered metallic lubber coated gaskets are used for the cylinder head gaskets.

ROCKER COVER




Aluminium alloy is used to reduce vibration and noise.

CYLINDER BLOCK




Cylinder block uses alloy cast iron.

PISTON




The piston uses special aluminum alloy to improves heat efficiency and achieve lower weight. This improves engine performance and fuel efficiency.
The piston pinhole center is offset by 1.3 mm from the piston center towards the thrust side. Oversized types of 0.50 mm and 1.00 mm are also available.
The piston skirt outside circumference is given striation-like finishing to get better oil holding property and superior endurance against burns

TIMING BELT




Timing belt is the cogged type, using rubber as its basic material, and it employs a high-strength glass fiber core which dose not expand or contract.
The features of the cogged-type belt compared to a chain are no expansion/contraction, low noise level, no necessity for lubrication light weight etc.

AUTO-TENSIONER




A mechanism that uses silicon oil to maintain timing belt tension, which would otherwise change with engine temperature or over time. It makes for reduced noise and enhanced belt durability.

VALVE MECHANISM




The valve mechanism is the high-speed compatible overhead valve (OHV), overhead camshaft (OHC) system the camshaft is driven by the crankshaft sprocket, cogged-type timing belt, and camshaft sprocket.
The camshaft is between the intake and exhaust valves, which are arranged as a V.
The valve spring is set between the cylinder head and spring retainer secured by the valve stem by the retainer lock, and is pressed against the valve seat of the cylinder head by spring tension.
When the camshaft rotates, the rocker arm is activated by the cam, using the rocker shaft as a fulcrum, thus opening and closing the valve.
The valve stem seal is installed at the upper edge of the valve guide, and functions to prevent the oil from going down in the clearance between the valve guide and the valve stem.

AUTO LASH ADJUSTER




These engines have auto adjusters that eliminate the need for valve clearance adjustment and reduce noises of the valve system. The auto lash adjuster automatically absorbs change in the valve clearance caused by engine temperature change or wear of parts and adjusts the valve clearance always to zero.

OPERATION OF AUTO LASH ADJUSTER

AT START OF VALVE LIFT
When the cam lifts up the rocker arm, the hydraulic pressure immediately forces the ball in the high pressure chamber against the seat, closing the high pressure camber.

DURING VALVE LIFT
A small amount of oil leaks from the high pressure chamber through a clearance between the body inside and the plunger outside.

AT END OF VALVE LIFT
The plunger springs forces the body back to the initial position and oil is supplied to the high pressure chamber to make up for the leaked oil.
