GENERAL INFORMATION


The rear axle has the following features:

  • The wheel bearing is a double tapered roller type unit bearing and is highly resistant to a thrust load.
  • The drive shaft and rear differential companion shaft are combined in a single unit for fewer parts and reduced the weight.
  • The EBJ-DOJ constant velocity joint is adopted for the drive shaft to reduce the weight.
  • The resin boot is adopted for the drive shaft boot to reduce the thermal and aging deterioration.
  • ABS rotors for detecting the wheel speed are press-fitted to the EBJ outer race.
  • Rear differential lock system has been adopted as optional.
note
    EBJ: High Efficiency Compact Birfield Joint: the lighter and smaller constant velocity joint compared with the conventional BJ has been achieved by adopting the eight small balls instead of six balls.
    DOJ: Double Offset Joint
