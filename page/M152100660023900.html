Inspection Procedure 1: The Automatic Anti-Dazzling Mirror does not Work normally.


























COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


The anti-glare function works automatically at night.

TROUBLESHOOTING HINTS



  • Malfunction of connector.
  • Malfunction of the inside rear view mirror assembly.

DIAGNOSIS

STEP 1. Connector check: E-101 automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector <Vehicles without MMCS>

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 2. Voltage measurement at E-101 automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 <Vehicles without MMCS> inside rear view mirror connector <Vehicles without MMCS>.


(1)Disconnect automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector E-101 <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 <Vehicles without MMCS, and measure the voltage at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between terminal 1 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. Check the wiring harness between E-101 automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 <Vehicles without MMCS> inside rear view mirror connector terminal No.1 and the ignition switch (IG2).
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connector D-130, junction block connectors D-410 and D-403, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 4. Resistance measurement E-101automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector <Vehicles without MMCS>.
(1)Disconnect the connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Measure the resistance value between terminal 2 <Vehicles with MMCS> or 4 <Vehicles without MMCS> and earth.
OK: Continuity (Less than 2 Ω)

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 5.

STEP 5. Check the wiring harness between E-101 automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector terminal No.2 <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector terminal No.4 <Vehicles without MMCS> and earth.

  • Check the earth wire for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 6. Voltage measurement at E-101automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector <Vehicles without MMCS>.
(1)Disconnect automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector E-101<Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector <Vehicles without MMCS> and measure the voltage at the harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Shift the selector lever to the "R" range. <A/T>
(4)Shift the lever to the "R" position. <M/T>
(5)Measure voltage between terminal 3 <Vehicles with MMCS> or 2 <Vehicles without MMCS> and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 17.
NO <A/T> : Go to Step 7.
NO <M/T> : Go to Step 12.

STEP 7. Connector check: C-05 inhibitor switch connector

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the damaged component(s). Refer to GROUP 00, Harness Connector Inspection .

STEP 8. Check the inhibitor switch.
Refer to GROUP 23A, Inhibitor Switch Continuity Check .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Replace the inhibitor switch.

STEP 9. Check the ignition switch (IG1) circuit to the inhibitor switch. Measure the voltage at inhibitor switch connector C-05.
(1)Disconnect inhibitor switch connector C-05 and measure the voltage available at the wiring harness side of the connector.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Measure voltage between terminal 3 <4M41> or 7 <6G7> and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Go to Step 10.

STEP 10. Check the wiring harness between C-05 inhibitor switch connector terminal No.3 <4M41> or No.7<6G7> and the ignition switch (IG1).
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-225 and C-229, junction block connectors D-401 and D-403, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
No action is necessary and testing is complete.
The wiring harness may be damaged or the connector(s) may have loose, corroded or damaged terminals, or terminals pushed back in the connector. Repair the wiring harness as necessary.

STEP 11. Check the wiring harness between E-101 automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector terminal No.3 <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector terminal No.2 <Vehicles without MMCS> and C-05 inhibitor switch connector terminal No.4 <4M41> or No.8 <6G7>.
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-130, D-225 and D-230, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 12. Connector check: C-17 back-up lamp switch connector

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Repair or replace the damaged component(s). Refer to GROUP 00, Harness Connector Inspection .

STEP 13. Check the back-up lamp switch.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair the back-up lamp switch.

STEP 14. Check the ignition switch (IG1) circuit to the backup light switch. Measure the voltage at back-up lamp switch connector C-17.
(1)Disconnect automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector E-101<Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140<Vehicles without MMCS> and measure the voltage at the harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Measure voltage between terminal 2 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Go to Step 15.

STEP 15. Check the wiring harness between C-17 back-up lamp switch connector terminal No.2 and the ignition switch (IG1).
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-225 and D-229, junction block connectors D-401and D-403, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
No action is necessary and testing is complete.
The wiring harness may be damaged or the connector(s) may have loose, corroded or damaged terminals, or terminals pushed back in the connector. Repair the wiring harness as necessary.

STEP 16. Check the wiring harness between E-101 automatic anti-dazzling mirror connector terminal No.3 <Vehicles with MMCS> or E-140 inside rear view mirror connector terminal No.2 <Vehicles without MMCS> and C-17 back-up lamp switch connector terminal No.1.
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-130, D-225 and D-230, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 17. Check the inside rear view mirror assembly.
Refer to .

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the inside rear view mirror assembly.
