Inspection Procedure 6: Broadcasts can be Heard, but Each of LW/MW/FM has a lot of Noise.


DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Check the state of the antenna.


Check that there is no damage or crack in the glass antenna, or the radio amplifier is assembled securely.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.

Repair or replace the glass antenna or radio amplifier.

STEP 2. Check that the noise occur when the engine is stopped or the engine is running.


Q. Does noise occur when the engine is stopped or the engine is running?

When the engine is stopped : Go to Step 3.

When the engine is running : Check the vehicle’s noise suppressor (Refer to Inspection Procedure 7 ).

STEP 3. Check that the following actions disappear the noise.


(1)Tune to a station with a stronger wave.
(2)Adjust the sound quality to suppress high tones.

Q. Is the noise eliminated?

This diagnosis is complete.

Go to Step 4.

STEP 4. Check that the radio and CD player or CD changer is correctly earthed


The radio and CD player or CD changer is connected to the earth with an assembling screw.

Q. Is the radio correctly earthed?

Go to Step 5.

Assemble the radio and CD player or CD changer into the vehicle.

STEP 5. Check the antenna plug is connected to the radio and CD player or CD changer.


Q. Is the antenna plug thoroughly connected to the radio and CD player or CD changer?

Consult the radio manufacturer’s service centre.

Go to Step 6.

STEP 6. Check that the noise is eliminated when the antenna plug is properly attached.


Q. Is the noise eliminated?

This diagnosis is complete.

Consult the radio manufacturer’s service centre.
