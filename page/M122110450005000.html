Code No.35: Free-wheel engage switch system
















OPERATION


The free-wheel engage switch is turned ON when the freewheel engage solenoid valve is activated and the condition is shifted to the 4WD condition. The power supply voltage is imposed to the free-wheel engage switch from the transfer-ECU, and when the free-wheel engage switch is turned ON, the power supply voltage from the transfer-ECU is grounded.

DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS


When the energizing condition to the free-wheel engage solenoid valve and the condition of free-wheel engage switch do not agree, code No.35 is set as the open circuit or the short circuit on the free-wheel engage switch system.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunction of the free-wheel engage switch
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the transfer-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code.
Check if the diagnosis code is set.

Q. Is diagnosis code 34 set?
Refer to diagnosis code 34: Free-wheel Engage Solenoid Valve System .
Go to Step 2.

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III data list.
Item 38: Free-wheel engage switch (Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. M.U.T.-III data list.
(1)Remove the free-wheel engage switch.
(2)Do not disconnect connector B-114.
(3)Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.
(4)Item 38: Free-wheel engage switch.
(5)When the free-wheel engage switch is released (OFF).
OK: M.U.T.-III display should be "OFF"
When the free-wheel engage switch is pressed (ON).
OK: M.U.T.-III display should be "ON"

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Go to Step 5.

STEP 4. Check the free-wheel engage solenoid valve.
Refer to GROUP 26 - On-vehicle Service .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Repair or replace the actuator assembly and free-wheel engage clutch (Refer to GROUP 26 - Differential Carrier and Free-wheel Clutch ).
Repair or replace the free-wheel engage solenoid valve and vacuum hoses (Refer to GROUP 26 - Solenoid Valve and Vacuum Hose ).

STEP 5. Check the free-wheel engage switch.
Refer to GROUP 26 - Differential Carrier and Free-wheel Clutch .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Replace the free-wheel engage switch (Refer to GROUP 26 - Differential Carrier and Free-wheel Clutch ).

STEP 6. Measure the voltage at freewheel engage switch connector B-114.
(1)Disconnect the connector, and measure the voltage between terminal No.2 and earth at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.
OK: 10.5 V

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Go to Step 7.

STEP 7. Measure the voltage at transfer-ECU connector D-123.
(1)Turn the ignition switch to the ON position.
(2)Transmission gear should be neutral position.
(3)Measure the voltage between transfer-ECU connector D-123 terminal No.50 and earth.
OK:

  • When the transfer shift lever position is 2H, voltage should measure 10.5 V.
  • When the transfer shift lever position is other than 2H, voltage should measure 1 V or less.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Go to Step 8.

STEP 8. Connector check: B-20, D-225 intermediate connector, B-114 free-wheel engage switch connector, D-123 transfer-ECU connector.
Check for the contact with terminals.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 9. Check the harness between transfer-ECU connector D-123 terminal No.50 and free-wheel engage switch connector B-114 terminal No.2.
Check the output line for short circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 10. Connector check: B-20, D-225 intermediate connector, B-114 free-wheel engage switch connector, D-123 transfer-ECU connector.
Check for the contact with terminals.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 11. Check the harness between transfer-ECU connector D-123 terminal No.50 and free-wheel engage switch connector B-114 terminal No.2.
Check the output line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 12. Measure the resistance at free-wheel engage switch connector B-114.
Disconnect the connector, and measure the resistance between terminal No.1 and earth at the wiring harness side.
OK: Continuity (2 Ω or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Go to Step 13.

STEP 13. Connector check: B-20 intermediate connector, B-114 free-wheel engage switch connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 14. Check the harness between free-wheel engage switch connector B-114 terminal No.1 and earth.
Check the earth line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 15. M.U.T.-III data list.
Item 38: Free-wheel engage switch (Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the transfer-ECU.
