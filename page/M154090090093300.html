Rear view camera image is not correctly displayed. <Vehicles without MMCS>


caution Before replacing the rear view camera or inside rear view mirror assembly, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit, and the communication circuit are normal.






























COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
When the screen of rear view camera is not shown even if the selector lever <A/T> or shift lever <M/T> is "R" (Reverse) position, the rear view camera, the inside rear view mirror assembly, the wiring harness, connectors, back-up lamp switch <M/T> or inhibitor switch <A/T> may be defective.
note If the indicator on the inside rear view mirror assembly is illuminated in orange but the monitor is not displayed, this is not a malfunction as the monitor has been set to always stay OFF.

PROBABLE CAUSES

  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the back-up lamp switch <MT>
  • Malfunction of the inhibitor switch <A/T>
  • Malfunction of the rear view camera
  • Malfunction of the inside rear view mirror assembly

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Connector check: E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2.
Repair or replace the connector.

STEP 2. Resistance measurement E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector.
(1)Disconnect E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector, and measure the resistance available at the wiring harness side of the connector.
(2)Check the continuity between terminal No. 4 and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. Check the wiring harness between E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector terminal No. 4 and body earth.

  • Check the earth wires for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 4. Voltage measurement at E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector.


(1)Disconnect E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector, and measure the voltage at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between terminal 1 and earth.
OK: system voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 5.

STEP 5. Check the wiring harness between E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector terminal No.1 and the ignition switch (IG2).
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connector D-130, junction block connectors D-410 and D-403, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 6. Voltage measurement at E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector.
(1)Disconnect E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector, and measure the voltage available at the wiring harness-side connector.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position, and move the selector lever <A/T> or shift lever <M/T> to the "R" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between terminal 2 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Go to Step 7.

STEP 7. Connector check: C-17 back-up switch connector <M/T> or C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T>

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 8. Check the harness wire between E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector terminal No.2 and C-17 back-up switch connector terminal No.1 <M/T> or C-05 inhibitor switch connector terminal No.3 <A/T: 3200> or No.7 <A/T: 3000, 3800>

  • Check the communication lines for open circuit and short circuit.
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-130, D-225 and D-230, and repair if necessary.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 9. Voltage measurement at C-17 back-up switch connector <M/T> or C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T>.
(1)Disconnect C-17 back-up switch connector <M/T> or C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T>, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between C-17 back-up switch connector terminal No. 2 <M/T> or C-05 inhibitor switch connector terminal No.3 <A/T: 3200> or No.7 <A/T: 3000, 3800> and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
YES <A/T> : Go to Step 11.
YES <M/T> : Go to Step 12.
Go to Step 10.

STEP 10. Check the harness wire between ignition switch (IG1) and C-17 back-up switch connector terminal No.2 <M/T> or C-05 inhibitor switch connector terminal No.3 <A/T: 3200> or No.7 <A/T: 3000, 3800>.

  • Check the communication lines for open circuit and short circuit.
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-229 and D-225 and joint connector D-401, D-403, and repair if necessary.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Troubleshoot the ignition switch. Refer to .
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 11. Check the inhibitor switch and the control cable.
Check that the inhibitor switch and the control cable are adjusted correctly.(GROUP 23A- On-vehicle Service, Essential Service, inhibitor switch and control cable adjustment <A/T: 3000, 3800> or GROUP 23C- On-vehicle Service, Essential Service, inhibitor switch and control cable adjustment <A/T: 3200>)

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Adjust the inhibitor switch and the control cable.

STEP 12. Check the inhibitor switch <A/T> or back-up lamp switch <M/T>.
Check the continuity of the inhibitor switch <A/T> or the back-up lamp switch <M/T>.(GROUP 23A - On-vehicle Service, Inhibitor Switch Continuity Check <A/T: 3000, 3800> or GROUP 23C - On-vehicle Service, Inhibitor Switch Continuity Check <A/T: 3200> or GROUP 22B - Transmission, Transmission Inspection <M/T>)

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the inhibitor switch <A/T> or back-up lamp switch <M/T>.

STEP 13. Connector check: G-10 rear view camera connector and E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 14. Check the wiring harness between G-10 rear view camera connector terminal No. 1, 3, 4, 6 and E-140 inside rear view mirror assembly connector terminal No. 5, 12, 6, 11.

  • Check the communication lines for open circuit and short circuit.
note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connectors D-130, D-221 and E-108, and repair if necessary.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 15. Trouble symptom recheck
Check that the display of rear view camera is shown normally.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the rear view camera temporarily and go to Step 16.

STEP 16. Trouble symptom recheck
Check that the display of rear view camera is shown normally.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Replace the rear view camera.
Replace the inside rear view mirror assembly.
