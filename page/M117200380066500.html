Code No. 22: Stop Lamp Switch System
















OPERATION



  • Battery positive voltage is supplied to the stop lamp switch (terminal No. 2 and No. 4).
  • When the brake pedal is depressed, battery positive voltage is applied to the engine-ECU <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU <A/T> terminal No. 39 and No. 54.

DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS


Check Condition
  • The cruise control indicator lamp illuminates.
Judgement Criteria
  • Open/short in stop lamp switch circuit.
  • Open circuit in the brake switch circuit (between engine-ECU <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU <A/T> terminal No. 54 and earth).

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Damaged harness or connector.
  • Malfunction of the stop lamp switch.
  • Malfunction of the engine-ECU <M/T>.
  • Malfunction of the engine-A/T-ECU <A/T>.

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list.

  • Item 74: Stop lamp switch
(Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Go to Step 2.

STEP 2. Check the stop lamp operation.
Check the stop lamp operation.
OK:

    Brake pedal depressed: Stop lamp will illuminate
    Brake pedal not depressed: Stop lamp does not illuminate

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 3.
Go to Step 5.

STEP 3. Connector check: D-244 engine-ECU connector <M/T> or D-214 engine-A/T-ECU connector <A/T>.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair or replace the damaged connector. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 4. Check the harness between D-244 engine-ECU connector <M/T> or D-214 engine-A/T-ECU connector <A/T> terminal No. 39 and stop lamp switch connector D-134 terminal No. 1.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 11.
Repair the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 5. Measure the voltage at stop lamp switch connector D-134.
(1)Disconnect stop lamp switch connector D-134.
(2)Measure the voltage between stop lamp switch connector D-134 terminal No. 2 and earth.
OK: System voltage
(3)Connect stop lamp switch connector D-134.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Go to Step 6.

STEP 6. Connector check: D-134 stop lamp switch connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair or replace the damaged connector. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 7. Check the harness between stop lamp switch connector D-134 terminal No. 2 and fuse No. 16 at the relay box in engine compartment.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 8. Check the fuse No. 16 at the relay box in engine compartment.

Q. Is the check result normal?
It can be assumed that this malfunction is intermittent (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Check the stop lamp system harness and replace the fuse. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 9. Check the harness between stop lamp switch connector D-134 terminal No. 1 and intermediate connector D-230 terminal No. 10.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Repair the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 10. Check the stop lamp switch.
(1)Remove the stop lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 35A - Brake Pedal ).
(2)Connect an ohmmeter to the stop lamp switch between terminals No. 1 and No. 2.

(3)Check for continuity between the terminals when the plunger of the stop lamp switch is pushed in and when it is released.
OK: The stop lamp switch is operating properly if the circuit is open between terminals No. 1 and No. 2 when the plunger is pushed in to a depth of within approximately 4.0 mm from the outer case edge surface, and if the resistance value is less than 2 ohms between terminals No. 1 and No. 2 when it is released.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Install the stop lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 35A - Brake Pedal ). Then go to Step 11.

Replace the stop lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 35A - Brake Pedal ). Then go to Step 20.

STEP 11. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list.

  • Item 74: Stop lamp switch
(Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 12.
Replace the engine-ECU <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU <A/T> (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU and Engine-A/T-ECU ) <6G72> or (Refer to GROUP 13B - Engine-ECU and Engine-A/T-ECU ) <6G75>. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 12. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list.

  • Item 89: Brake switch
(Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Go to Step 13.

STEP 13. Measure the voltage at engine-ECU connector D-244 <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU connector D-214.
(1)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(2)Measure the voltage between engine-ECU connector D-244 <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU connector D-214 <A/T> terminal No. 54 and earth.
OK:

    Brake pedal depressed: System voltage
    Brake pedal not depressed: 1 V or less
(3)Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 18.
Go to Step 14.

STEP 14. Connectors check: D-134 stop lamp switch connector and D-247 engine-ECU connector <M/T> or D-218 engine-A/T-ECU connector <A/T>.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair or replace the damaged connectors. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 15. Check the harness between engine-ECU connector D-247 <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU connector D-218 <A/T> terminal No. 54 and stop lamp switch connector D-134 terminal No. 4.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Repair the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 16. Check the harness between stop lamp switch connector D-134 terminal No. 3 and earth.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 17.
Repair the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 17. Check the stop lamp switch.
(1)Remove the stop lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 35A - Brake Pedal ).
(2)Connect an ohmmeter to the stop lamp switch between terminals No. 3 and No. 4.

(3)Check for continuity between the terminals when the plunger of the stop lamp switch is pushed in and when it is released.
OK: The stop lamp switch is operating properly if the circuit is open between terminals No. 3 and No. 4 when the plunger is released, and if resistance value is less than 2 ohms between terminals No. 3 and No. 4 when the plunger is pushed in to a depth of within approximately 4.5 mm from the outer case edge surface.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Install the stop lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 35A - Brake Pedal ). Then go to Step 18.

Replace the stop lamp switch (Refer to GROUP 35A - Brake Pedal ). Then go to Step 20.

STEP 18. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list.

  • Item 89: Brake switch
(Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Replace the engine-ECU <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU <A/T> (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU and Engine-A/T-ECU ) <6G72> or (Refer to GROUP 13B - Engine-ECU and Engine-A/T-ECU ) <6G75>. Then go to Step 20.

STEP 19. Read the diagnosis code.
(1)Disconnect the negative battery terminal, and erase the diagnosis code of the cruise control system.
(2)Connect the negative battery terminal.
(3)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position, and press the "ON/OFF" switch to turn the cruise control system to ON.
(4)With the cruise control switches not operated, depress the brake pedal for several seconds, and then read the diagnosis code of the cruise control system (Refer to ).

Q. Is diagnosis code No. 22 set?
Replace the engine-ECU <M/T> or engine-A/T-ECU <A/T> (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-ECU and Engine-A/T-ECU ) <6G72> or (Refer to GROUP 13B - Engine-ECU and Engine-A/T-ECU ) <6G75>. Then go to Step 20.
It can be assumed that this malfunction is intermittent (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

STEP 20. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list .

  • Item 74: Stop lamp switch
  • Item 89: Brake switch
(Refer to data list reference table ).

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 21.
Return to Step 2.

STEP 21. Read the diagnosis code.
(1)Disconnect the negative battery terminal, and erase the diagnosis code of the cruise control system.
(2)Connect the negative battery terminal.
(3)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position, and press the "ON/OFF" switch to turn the cruise control system to ON.
(4)With the cruise control switches not operated, depress the brake pedal for several seconds, and then read the diagnosis code of the cruise control system (Refer to ).

Q. Is diagnosis code No. 22 set?
Return to Step 1.
The procedure is complete.
