GENERAL INFORMATION


FEATURES


The brake system has been designed to give greater reliability and durability and to provide excellent braking performance.
BRAKING PERFORMANCE
  • A 8+9 inch tandem brake booster has been adopted to provide sufficient braking force in sudden braking range. <Vehicles without ABS>
  • Adoption of a hydraulic brake booster (HBB) which maintains a high brake-assisting force even in high deceleration ranges. <Except vehicles without ABS>
  • For the stable braking force and improvement of brake feeling, the 16-inch ventilated disc brake has been equipped for the short wheelbase and long wheelbase - 2800, 3000, and the 17-inch ventilated disc brake for the long wheelbase - 3200, 3800.
STABILITY
  • A 4-wheel anti-skid braking system (4ABS) has been adopted to prevent slipping caused by the vehicle wheels locking up, in order to maintain an appropriate braking distance, and also to maintain a stable vehicle posture and steering performance. <Except vehicles without ABS>
  • Adoption of the Active Stability and Traction Control System (ASTC) which integrates the Active Stability Control System with the Active Traction Control System: The Active Stability Control System responds to driving conditions and reduces the engine output and applies braking force to four wheels independently to control the vehicle behaviour, avoiding the critical state; The Active Traction Control System prevents the driving force loss during slipping of the driving wheels by automatically applying the brakes when driving on partly low-mu road surfaces such as off-road or when the driving wheels slip, and allows the vehicle to be rocked out of a hollow or a slippery surface, just like the differential lock. <Vehicles with ASTC>
  • Adoption of an electronic brake-force distribution(EBD) which makes it possible to maintain the maximum amount of braking force even when the vehicle’s load is unevenly distributed. <Except vehicles without ABS>
  • According to the fluctuations in the amount of load, load sensing proportioning valve (LSPV) is adopted to control the brake fluid pressure from the master cylinder fairly and prevent the early-lock of rear wheels, improving the stability during braking. <Vehicles without ABS>
  • Adoption of independent front and rear wheel brake lines (H piping).
  • Ventilated discs have been adopted for front wheel and rear wheel in order to improve anti-fading performance.
SERVICEABILITY
  • A diagnosis function has been adopted for the HBB and ABS system in order to make inspection easier. <Except vehicles without ABS>
  • For the front and rear disc brakes, an outer disc method separated hub and rotor has been adopted to make removal and installation easier.
