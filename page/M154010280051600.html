Inspection Procedure 5: The Auto Lamp Function does not Work Normally. <Not Using SWS Monitor>


caution Before replacing the ECU, ensure that the power supply circuit, the earth circuit and the communication circuit are normal.












OPERATION


The front-ECU operates this function based on the input signals from the ignition switch, ETACS-ECU, column switch (lighting switch), and lighting control sensor.

COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM


Based on the premise that the headlamp operates normally when the column switch (lighting switch) is in the "HEAD" position, if the auto lamp function does not work normally, the SWS communication line, LIN bus line, column switch, front-ECU, CAN converter-ECU, or lighting control sensor may have a problem.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Malfunction of the lighting control sensor
  • Malfunction of the CAN converter-ECU
  • Malfunction of the column switch
  • Malfunction of the front-ECU
  • Damaged harness wires and connectors

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE


STEP 1. Check that the headlamps operate.


Check that the low-beam and high-beam headlamps work normally.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 2.

Repair the headlamps. Refer to Trouble Symptom Chart .

STEP 2. M.U.T.-III diagnosis code


Check if diagnosis code is set to the CAN converter-ECU and the LIN.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Refer to diagnosis code chart .

Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. M.U.T.-III other system diagnosis code


Check if diagnosis code is set to the ETACS-ECU.

Q. Is the diagnosis code set?

Troubleshoot the ETACS-ECU (Refer to ).

Go to Step 4.

STEP 4. Pulse check


Check that the ETACS-ECU receives signals from the ignition switch and column switch (lighting switch).
System switch
 Check condition
Ignition switch (IG1)
 When turned from "ACC" to "ON"
Column switch (lighting switch)
 When the lighting switch is turned to the "AUTO" position
OK: The M.U.T.-III sounds or the voltmeter needle fluctuates.

Q. Are the check result normal?

YES (All the signals are received normally) : Go to Step 5.

NO (The ignition switch (IG1) signal is not received) : Refer to ETACS - Input Signal Chart .

NO (The column switch (lighting switch) signal is not received) : Refer to ETACS - Input Signal Chart .

STEP 5. Connector check: E-136 Lighting control sensor connector


Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 6.

Repair the defective connector.

STEP 6. Resistance measurement at the E-136 lighting control sensor connector


(1)Disconnect the lighting control sensor connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Measure the resistance between E-136 lighting control sensor connector terminal No.2 and body earth.
OK: Continuity exists (2 Ω or less)

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 8.

Go to Step 7.

STEP 7. Check the wiring harness between E-136 lighting control sensor connector terminal No.2 and body earth.



  • Check the earth wires for open circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?

The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 8. Voltage measurement at the E-136 lighting control sensor connector.


(1)Disconnect the lighting control sensor connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Measure the voltage between E-136 lighting control sensor connector terminal No. 1 and body earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 10.

Go to Step 9.

STEP 9. Check the wiring harness between E-136 lighting control sensor connector terminal No. 1 and fusible link (1).


note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connector D-241, D-130 and joint connector D-34, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the power supply line for open circuit and short circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?

The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).

Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 10. Connector check: D-252 CAN converter-ECU connector


Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 11.

Repair the defective connector.

STEP 11. Voltage measurement at the E-136 lighting control sensor connector.


(1)Disconnect the lighting control sensor connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch from "LOCK" (OFF) to "ON" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between E-136 lighting control sensor connector terminal No. 3 and body earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?

Replace the Lighting control sensor.

Go to Step 12.

STEP 12. Check the wiring harness wires between D-252 CAN converter-ECU terminal No.12 and E-136 lighting control sensor connector terminal No.3.


note Prior to the wiring harness inspection, check intermediate connector D-130, and repair if necessary.

  • Check the communication lines for open circuit and short circuit.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 13.

Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 13. Check the windshield surface to which the lighting control sensor is installed.


Check if any dirt or seal adheres to the windshield surface to which the lighting control sensor is installed.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Go to Step 14.

After removing the dirt or seal, go to Step 15.

STEP 14. Lighting control sensor removal and installation


Remove the lighting control sensor (refer to ), and check if there is any dust or dirt on the lighting control sensor receiver and the optical coupler.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Install the lighting control sensor (refer to ), and execute the lighting control sensor (rain sensor) adaptation. Then go to Step 15.

After removing the dust or dirt, install the lighting control sensor (refer to ), and execute the lighting control sensor (rain sensor) adaptation. Then go to Step 15.

STEP 15. Retest the system


Check that the auto lamp function works normally.

Q. Is the check result normal?

The diagnosis is complete.

Go to Step 16.

STEP 16. Temporarily replace the lighting control sensor, and check the trouble symptom.


Temporarily replace the lighting control sensor, and check that the auto lamp function works normally.

Q. Is the check result normal?

Replace the lighting control sensor.

Replace the CAN converter-ECU.
