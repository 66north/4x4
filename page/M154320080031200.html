Inspection Procedure 6: Obstacle is not detected even when the selector or shift lever is shifted to "R" (Reverse) position.


caution
  • Whenever the ECU is replaced, ensure that the input signal circuit is normal.
  • When the illuminated licence plate is installed, the sensors may not operate because of noise from the plate. (When the ultrasonic noise is received, the bass toned buzzer sound continuously. When the ultrasonic noise stops, the normal operation will be resumed immediately.)
















COMMENTS ON TROUBLE SYMPTOM
If obstacle is not detected even when the selector lever is shifted to "R" (reverse) position, the wiring harness, connectors, inhibitor switch <A/T>, back-up lamp switch <M/T>, or corner sensor-ECU may be defective. In addition, obstacle is not detected when the ultrasonic noise is received.
note If ultrasonic noise is received with the sensor, the corner sensor -ECU sounds the low toned buzzer continuously and turns off the system. When the ultrasonic noise is stopped, the sensor returns to normal.

PROBABLE CAUSES

  • Damaged harness wires and connectors
  • Malfunction of the inhibitor switch <A/T> or back-up lamp switch <M/T>
  • Malfunction of corner sensor-ECU

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Inspect the SONAR OFF indicator with ignition switch turned to "ON" position.
Verify that the SONAR OFF indicator will illuminate for 1 second when the ignition switch is turned to the "ON" position.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 2
Perform troubleshooting for the power supply circuit of the reversing sensor system. (Refer to .)

STEP 2. Check the reversing sensor (corner sensor/back sensor) for interference.

Q. Are there anything that interferes with the communication (things that generate radio waves such as magnets and an air-cleaning device that has a power plug) near the reversing sensor (corner sensor/back sensor)?
Move away or remove things that interfere with the communication (things that generate radio waves such as magnets and a air-cleaning device that has a power plug) near the reversing sensor (corner sensor/back sensor).
Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. Connector check: D-16 corner sensor-ECU connector and C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T> or C-17 back-up lamp switch connector <M/T>.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 4.
Repair the defective connector.

STEP 4. Voltage measurement at D-16 corner sensor-ECU connector.
(1)Disconnect D-16 corner sensor-ECU connector, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position, and move the selector lever <A/T> or the shift lever <M/T> to the "R" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between terminal No. 17 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9
Go to Step 5

STEP 5. Check the wiring harness between C-05 inhibitor switch connector (terminal No. 4 <3800> or terminal No. 8 <3200>) <A/T> or C-17 back-up lamp switch connector terminal No. 1 <M/T> and D-16 corner sensor-ECU connector terminal No. 17.

  • Check the wiring harness for open circuit and short circuit.
note Before the wiring harness inspection, inspect the intermediate connectors D-225, and D-230, and repair if necessary.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 6. Voltage measurement at C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T> or C-17 back-up lamp switch connector <M/T>.
(1)Disconnect C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T> or C-17 back-up lamp switch connector <M/T>, and measure at the wiring harness side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to "ON" position.
(3)Measure the voltage between or C-05 inhibitor switch connector <A/T> (terminal No. 3 <3800> or terminal No. 7 <3200>) or C-17 back-up lamp switch connector <M/T> terminal No. 2 and earth.
OK: System voltage

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8
Go to Step 7

STEP 7. Check the wiring harness between C-05 inhibitor switch connector (terminal No. 3 <3800> or terminal No. 7 <3200>) <A/T> or C-17 back-up lamp switch connector terminal No. 2 <M/T> and the ignition switch (IG1).

  • Check the communication line for open circuit or short circuit.
note Before the wiring harness inspection, inspect the intermediate connectors D-225, D-229, and junction block connector D-401, D-403, and repair if necessary.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Troubleshoot the ignition switch. Refer to .
Repair the wiring harness.

STEP 8. Check the inhibitor switch <A/T> or back-up lamp switch <M/T>.
Check the continuity of the inhibitor switch <A/T> or the back-up lamp switch <M/T>.(GROUP 23A - On-vehicle Service, Inhibitor Switch Continuity check <A/T>, GROUP 22B - Transmission, Transmission Inspection <M/T>)

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9
Replace the inhibitor switch <A/T> or back-up lamp switch <M/T>.

STEP 9. Retest the system
Check if an obstacle is detected when the selector lever is shifted to the "R" (Reverse) position.

Q. Is the check result normal?
The trouble can be an intermittent malfunction (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to use Troubleshooting/inspection Service Points - How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunction ).
Replace the corner sensor-ECU.
