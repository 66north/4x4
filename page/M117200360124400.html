Code No.15 Cruise Control Switch System.












OPERATION


This circuit judges the signals of each switch ("ON/OFF", "CANCEL", "COAST/SET" and "ACC/RES") of the cruise control switch. The engine-A/T-ECU detects the state of the cruise control switch by sensing the voltages shown below.

  • When all switches are OFF: 4.7 - 5.0 volts.
  • When the "ON/OFF" switch is ON: 0 - 0.5 volt.
  • When the "CANCEL" switch is ON: 1.0 - 1.8 volts.
  • When the "COAST/SET" switch is ON: 2.3 - 3.0 volts.
  • When the "ACC/RES" switch is ON: 3.5 - 4.2 volts.

DIAGNOSIS CODE SET CONDITIONS



  • If the cruise control switch is operated, this diagnosis code will be set when the engine-A/T-ECU terminal voltage is different from the standard value.
  • Or, this code is set when the "COAST/SET" switch or "ACC/RES" switch is stuck to ON.

PROBABLE CAUSES



  • Damaged harness or connector.
  • Malfunction of the cruise control switch.
  • Malfunction of the clock spring.
  • Malfunction of the engine-A/T-ECU.

DIAGNOSIS PROCEDURE

STEP 1. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list (Refer to - data list reference table.)

  • Item 75: "CANCEL" switch.
  • Item 86: "ON/OFF" switch.
  • Item 91: "ACC/RES" switch.
  • Item 92: "COAST/SET" switch.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 20.
Go to Step 2.

STEP 2. Measure the terminal voltage at cruise control switch connector D-301.
(1)Remove the driver’s air bag module [Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s, Front Passenger’s Air Bag Module(s) and Clock Spring .]
(2)Remove the cruise control switch from the steering wheel with the cruise control switch connector connected (Refer to .)
(3)Connect the negative battery cable that was disconnected when the driver’s air bag module was removed.
(4)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(5)Do not operate the cruise control switch.
(6)Measure the terminal voltage between cruise control switch connector D-301 terminal No.4 and earth with cruise control switch connector D-301 connected.
OK: 4.7 - 5.0 V

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 10.
Go to Step 3.

STEP 3. Measure the terminal voltage at engine-A/T-ECU connector D-217.
(1)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(2)Do not operate the cruise control switch.
(3)Measure the terminal voltage between engine-A/T-ECU connector D-217 terminal No.94 and earth with engine-A/T-ECU connector connected.
OK: 4.7 - 5.0 V

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 6.
Go to Step 4.

STEP 4. Connector check: D-217 engine-A/T-ECU connector.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 5.
Repair or replace the damaged connector. Then go to Step 21.

STEP 5. Check the harness for short circuit to earth between engine-A/T-ECU connector D-217 terminal No.94 and cruise control switch connector D-301 terminal No.4.
(1)Disconnect engine-A/T-ECU connector D-217 and measure at the harness connector side.
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "LOCK" (OFF) position.
(3)Measure the continuity between engine-A/T-ECU connector D-217 terminal No.94 and earth.
OK: open circuit

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Go to Step 6.

STEP 6. Connectors check: D-301 cruise control switch connector, D-229 intermediate connector, D-302 and D-305 clock spring connectors.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 7.
Repair or replace the damaged connector. Then go to Step 21.

STEP 7. Check the harness wire between engine-A/T-ECU connector D-217 terminal No.94 and clock spring connector D-305 terminal No.5, and between clock spring connector D-302 terminal No.2 and cruise control switch connector D-301 terminal No.4.

  • Check harness wire for open/short circuit and damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 8.
Repair or replace the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 21.

STEP 8. Check the clock spring.
Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s, Front Passenger’s Air Bag Module(s) and Clock Spring Inspection .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 9.
Replace the clock spring and install the driver’s air bag module [Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s, Front Passenger’s Air Bag Module(s) and Clock Spring .] Then go to Step 21.

STEP 9. Check the data list.
Using M.U.T.-III, check the data list (Refer to - data list reference table.)

  • Item 75: "CANCEL" switch.
  • Item 86: "ON/OFF" switch.
  • Item 91: "ACC/RES" switch.
  • Item 92: "COAST/SET" switch.

Q. Is the check result normal?
It can be assumed that this malfunction is intermittent (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions .)
Replace the engine-A/T-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-A/T-ECU .) Then go to Step 21.

STEP 10. Measure the terminal voltage at cruise control switch connector D-301.
(1)Remove the cruise control switch from the steering wheel with the cruise control switch connector connected (Refer to .)
(2)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(3)Press the "ON/OFF" switch with cruise control switch connector D-301 connected, and measure the terminal voltage between cruise control switch connector D-301 terminal No.3 and earth.
OK: 0.5 V or less

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 17.
Go to Step 11.

STEP 11. Measure the terminal voltage at engine-A/T-ECU connector D-218.
(1)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position.
(2)Press the "ON/OFF" switch, and measure the terminal voltage between engine-A/T-ECU connector D-218 terminal No.146 and earth with engine-A/T-ECU connector connected.
OK: 0.5 V or less

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 13.
Go to Step 12.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 19.
Repair or replace the damaged connector. Then go to Step 21.

STEP 13. Connectors check: D-218 engine-A/T-ECU connector, D-229 intermediate connector, D-301 cruise control switch connector, D-302 and D-305 clock spring connectors.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 14.
Repair or replace the damaged connector. Then go to Step 21.

STEP 14. Check the harness wire between engine-A/T-ECU connector D-218 terminal No.146 and clock spring connector D-305 terminal No.4, and between clock spring connector D-302 terminal No.3 and cruise control switch connector D-301 terminal No.3.

  • Check harness wire for open/short circuit and damage.

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 15.
Repair or replace the damaged harness wire. Then go to Step 21.

STEP 15. Check the clock spring.
Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s, Front Passenger’s Air Bag Module(s) and Clock Spring Inspection .

Q. Is the check result normal?
Go to Step 16.
Replace the clock spring and install the driver’s air bag module [Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s, Front Passenger’s Air Bag Module(s) and Clock Spring .] Then go to Step 21.

  • Item 75: "CANCEL" switch.
  • Item 86: "ON/OFF" switch.
  • Item 91: "ACC/RES" switch.
  • Item 92: "COAST/SET" switch.

Q. Is the check result normal?
It can be assumed that this malfunction is intermittent (Refer to GROUP 00 - How to Use Troubleshooting/Inspection Service Points, How to Cope with Intermittent Malfunctions .) Install the driver’s air bag module [Refer to GROUP 52B - Driver’s, Front Passenger’s Air Bag Module(s) and Clock Spring .]

Q. Is the check result normal?

Refer to .

Go to Step 20.
Replace the engine-A/T-ECU (Refer to GROUP 13A - Engine-A/T-ECU .) Then go to Step 21.

STEP 20. Read the diagnosis code.
(1)Disconnect the negative battery terminal, and erase the diagnosis code of the cruise control system.
(2)Connect the negative battery terminal.
(3)Turn the ignition switch to the "ON" position, and press the "ON/OFF" switch to turn the cruise control system to ON (turn ON the cruise control indicator lamp.)
(4)After turning the cruise control system to ON, when 2 minutes or more has elapsed without operating the cruise control switches, read the diagnosis code of the cruise control system (Refer to .)
