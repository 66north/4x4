ABS-ECU



  • The ABS-ECU detects vehicle speed and deceleration speed from the wheel speed sensor and G sensor signals, recognises the wheel rotation, estimates the wheel slip condition based on the preprogrammed logic, and then controls the brake pressure by controlling the solenoid valve in the hydraulic brake booster (HBB).
  • Self-diagnostic programs and memory functions are integrated into ABS-ECU. If any malfunction is detected by the self-diagnostic function, it activates a fail-safe function and illuminates the ABS warning lamp and brake warning lamp.

ABS HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL




ABS control cycle
  1. ABS-ECU calculates the speed and deceleration of each wheel based on the signals from the four wheel speed sensors and G sensor, and estimates the vehicle speed at that time.
  2. When the brake pedal is depressed, the brake fluid pressure applied to the wheel cylinder increases, and the wheel speed decreases. When the difference between the wheel speed and vehicle speed increases, and the vehicle deceleration goes below the specified value (Point A), ECU determines that the wheels are about to be locked. At this time, ECU reduces the brake fluid pressure by outputting the pressure decrease signal to the control solenoid valves (IN, OUT). (between a and b)
  3. When the vehicle deceleration and wheel speed begin recovery, and the vehicle speed reaches the point B, ECU outputs the pressure hold signal to maintain the wheel cylinder fluid pressure. (between b and c)
  4. When the wheel speed deceleration is further recovered and overpasses the point C, ECU determines that the wheel lock possibility has been eliminated and maintains the brake fluid pressure by outputting the pressure increase signal again. (between c and d)
  5. Brake fluid pressure is controlled by repeating the increase and hold of the pressure. (between d and e)
  6. When the wheel deceleration goes below the threshold again, ABS-ECU controls the brake fluid pressure by repeating the cycle (Step 2 to 5).
Four-wheel control
  1. The 4-sensor system that controls the four wheels independently is adopted.
  2. ABS is available with the vehicle speed of approximately 10 km/h or more.

EBD HYDRAULIC PRESSURE CONTROL





EBD control is activated in a range with lower slip ratio where ABS is disabled. EBD calculates vehicle deceleration and slip amount of the four wheels based on the wheel speed sensor signal. If the rear wheel speed differs from the vehicle speed by a certain level or more, EBD increases, holds, and decreases the pressure at the rear wheel control solenoid valve in the HBB, and then adjusts rear wheel brake fluid pressure fairly close to an ideal distribution curve.

INITIAL CHECK


The ABS-ECU performs the initial check (self-diagnostic function) using the diagnostic function. It illuminates the ABS warning lamp for approximately 3 seconds (including the initial check) * after the ignition switch is turned ON. If any malfunction is detected, it continues illuminating the ABS warning lamp and disables ABS control.
note *: The ABS warning lamp may stay on after the ignition switch is turned ON until the startup vehicle speed reaches approximately 8 km/h. As far as ABS-ECU memorises any diagnosis code related to the wheel speed sensor recorded during the previous ignition switch ON status, ABS-ECU continues illuminating the ABS warning lamp until it verifies that the malfunction for that code is resolved (startup check).

INITIAL CHECK OPERATION





  1. When the ignition switch is turned to the ON position, power is supplied to the ABS-ECU, and the ABS-ECU internal self-check is performed.
  2. Then the ABS-ECU compares the drive signal with monitor value for all the solenoid valves, and checks that the solenoid valves are OFF. (With this check, the solenoid valves are not energised.)
  3. When the ignition switch is turned to the START position, the power supply to ABS-ECU is shut off, and the ABS warning lamp is turned ON.
  4. When the ignition switch is returned to the ON position after engine startup, the power is supplied to the ABS-ECU, and the initial check steps 1 and 2 are performed again.
  5. When the initial check is completed normally, the ABS can activate.

STARTING-OFF CHECK


When the vehicle speed becomes approximately 8 km/h at the initial start after the system startup (while the ABS and EBD are not controlled), the ABS-ECU energises the solenoid valves sequentially for a very short time and checks the valves electrically.

CONSTANT CHECKS


ABS-ECU constantly checks the following items.
1.ABS-ECU
Performs self-diagnosis in ECU.
2.ABS-ECU power supply
Checks if ABS-ECU power supply voltage stays within the operational range.
3.Valve relay
(1) Checks if valve relay monitor voltage stays within the operational range.
(2) Checks that the ABS-ECU output signal (ON signal) and the operating conditions of the valve relay (ON) agree with each other.
4.Wheel speed sensor
(1) Monitors the output voltage of the sensor signal wiring harness and checks for abnormal output voltage (open/short circuit).
(2) Checks if other wheel speeds are input normally when the speed of one or more wheels are approximately 8 km/h or more.
(3) Checks for any wheels that suddenly decelerates or do not send pulse signal while the vehicle is driving.
(4) Checks if wheel speed which is abnormally higher or lower than the vehicle speed is input when the vehicle speed is 20 km/h or more.
5.G sensor
(1) Checks for abnormal output voltage of G sensor, and detects open or short circuit in the G sensor.
(2) Checks the output voltage of G sensor, compares with the stop lamp signal and wheel speed signal, and then determines that the G sensor is stuck when the voltage does not change for a certain period of time.
6.Pump motor, switching solenoid valve, and control solenoid valve
Checks that the ABS-ECU output signal and the operating conditions of the pump motor and solenoid valve agree with each other.

FAIL-SAFE DIAGNOSIS FUNCTION


If any malfunction is detected by the self-diagnostic function, the ABS-ECU illuminates the ABS warning lamp and brake warning lamp*, and it disables ABS and EBD control.
note *: The brake warning lamp is used as EBD control warning lamp.

DIAGNOSTIC FUNCTION


ABS-ECU has the following functions for easier system checks. All of the following items can be diagnosed using the M.U.T.-III.

  • Diagnosis code set
  • Service data output
  • Actuator test

DIAGNOSIS CODE OUTPUT


There are 24 diagnostic items. Since all the diagnostic results are recorded in volatile memory (EEPROM*), they are stored in the memory even though the battery terminals are disconnected.
note
  • *: EEPROM (Electrical Erasable & Programmable ROM): Special type of memory that can be programmed or erased electrically.
  • For each diagnostic item, refer to Workshop Manual.
